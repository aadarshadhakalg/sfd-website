Software Freedom Day

Software Freedom Day (SFD) is an annual global celebration of Free and Open Source Software(FOSS). It is celebrated with the aim to educate the public and increase awareness about Free software, its benifits and encouraging its use.Since its inception in 28 August, 2004 with about 12 participating team, the community has steadily grown to the current day where over 300 events are organized by over 100 cities from across the world.

Free And Open Software

Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) refers to software that anyone is freely licensed to use, copy, study and change in any way that they want, and the source code is openly shared so that people are encouraged to volunterily improve the design of the software. Use of free software encourages learning and sharing what we have learned with others in the community. Other benifits of using FOSS would be to decreased software cost, protecting privecy and providing more control to the users.

KUOSC

Kathmandu University Open Source Community (KUOSC) is the Open Source related wing of KUCC. It is a vibrant historic community from Kathmandu University which provides a common platform for all FOSS enthusiasts to discuss, share, and learn about free and open source softwares. KUOSC is specifically focused on all the Open Source related activities ranging from training programs like Linux Talks, Useful Network Training to advocacy activities like Software Freedom Day celebration and organizing different community level workshops and events.
